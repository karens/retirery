Why Work When You're Retired?

Earning a small salary during retirement is the equivalent of having a larger retirement nest egg to live off of. It reduces or even eliminates the need to dip into retirement investments by providing money to pay day-to-day expenses, or to offset the costs of a trip or other big ticket items. If you're worried about whether you have enough saved up to live off of for the rest of your life, a retirement job provides a cushion to ease your mind.

There are lots of other reasons retirees might want or need to work in retirement. A job gives a person a sense of structure, a reason to get up in the morning, and a way to stay connected to other people.

But if you go back to working full time, you're no longer retired. A good retirement job is different than a regular job. A retirement job doesn't have to provide enough income to fully support you, just enough to supplement your Social Security or pension, so instead of focusing on pay, you can choose jobs based on how they fit into your schedule or select jobs that allow you to make a difference in something you care about. A good retirement job is flexible. It might be part-time year-round or full-time on a seasonal basis, with long breaks to do other things.

Finding good retirement jobs might be challenging. Many traditional job services are focused on traditional jobs. Not all employers are open to part-time, flexible, work, and they may worry about the motives of older job applicants not looking for full-time work. In some cases, the best retirement job may be working for yourself in some capacity, by turning a repair skill into a small business or selling crafts at flea markets.

The Retirery is a place to look for retirement job ideas, a factory, of sorts, of those ideas, along with links to resources and other ways to take action on them.
