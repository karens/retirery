Involuntary Early Retirement

An AARP survey reports that 46% of retirees retired earlier than they originally planned. When listing reasons for the early retirement, early retirees listed many that were involuntary or unexpected.

The survey talked to people age 50+, divided between those who are already retired and those who are not yet retired, to try to assess their feelings about retirement. Of the 46% of retirees who retired earlier than planned, the primary reasons for doing so were involuntary.

The involuntary reasons included

  • Personal health problems
  • The need to care for someone else (someone else's health problems)
  • Job layoffs or downsizing
  • Changes in the skills required to do their job

Other reasons for retiring earlier than planned included:

  • Other job-related reasons (possibly negative things happening at work)
  • Wanted to do something else (some of these could also be a reaction to stress or problems at work)
  • Had enough money to retire

One possible outcome of an earlier than expected early retirement is that they won't have enough money set aside to live on. Finding supplemental sources of income in retirement makes a lot of sense for those that unexpectedly retired early.

You can't wait too long to start planning for retirement, it may happen sooner than you expect.

Source: AARP's 2016 Retirement Confidence Survey
